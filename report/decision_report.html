Recurrence Decision Report

1. Executive Summary

This report presents the result of a recurrence prediction pipeline, including model outputs, validation checks, and decision-support segmentation. The key highlights are listed below:

  • Recurrence risk was estimated using logistic regression models
  • The population was segmented into risk tiers (high, medium, low)
  • Suggested actions were assigned to support prioritisation
  • Validation checks were performed to ensure output reliability

5. Interpretation

The model outputs are intended to support prioritisation decisions rather than to provide deterministic predictions.

Here are key considerations:

  • Risk tiers are based on probability thresholds and should be interpreted alongside contextual information.

  • High-risk cases indicate higher likelihood of recurrence within the predefined time window.

  • The models are designed for demonstration using simulated data and does not represent real-world intervention effectiveness.

6. Limitations

The project has limitations listed below:

  • The data used in this work is simulated.
  • Recurrence is driven primarily by risk level in the data generating process.
  • Duration-based features may not reflect real-world signals.
  • Thresholds for risk segmentation are illustrative.
